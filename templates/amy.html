Hello, internet friends. My name is Amy. I’m a Bay Area-bred, New York-based actor and human. I originally moved to New York to attend Barnard College, where I graduated magna cum laude with a degree in English and Theatre. As part of my senior thesis I managed to force my way into Amy Morton’s dressing room and interview her about playing Martha in Who’s Afraid of Virginia Woolf on Broadway.

Since graduation I’ve performed at various off- and off-off-Broadway theaters in the city, including The Connelly Theater, Robert Moss Theater, and Theater for the New City. You can also see me starring in an episode of Millennials of New York – an Elite Daily-produced web series parodying Humans of New York.

Most recently I completed The Studio / New York’s Nine Month Conservatory program, where I studied with some of the best teachers I could have ever hoped to meet, including Jayd McCarty, Lucas Caleb Rooney, Fay Simpson, Sita Mani, and Ilse Pfeifer. I’m now re-entering the acting world with a straighter spine, a stronger voice, and a humble approach to the stories that I am excited to share.

In my spare time I enjoy taking naps in the city’s various parks, learning new flying trapeze tricks, or daydreaming about what it would be like to live by myself and have a cat.
